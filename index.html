About ZenLink

Built from inside the problem

ZenLink was founded on a simple observation: most enterprise software sold to Nigerian institutions was built somewhere else, for someone else. The workflows don't match. The pricing doesn't fit. The offline support is an afterthought.

We build from scratch, starting with the actual problem. Our process is grounded in direct observation, stakeholder conversations, and a close understanding of how institutions actually operate. That proximity helps us design systems that fit the context from the beginning.

Our focus is three verticals where software gaps cause the most friction: education administration, health information, and agricultural supply chains. Each area is grounded in specific institutional problems and designed to solve them without overcomplicating the experience.

Context-first design
Every solution starts with how Nigerians actually work, not how Western software assumes they do.
Real-time, trustworthy data
Our systems deliver live information that administrators can rely on and act on immediately.
Institutional adoption
We build for administrators and decision-makers, not just end-users. Buy-in from leadership is part of the implementation strategy.
Architected to scale
Our systems are designed for multi-institution deployment from day one, not bolted on later.
Focus Areas

Three verticals where execution matters most

ZenLink is organised around three operating domains: education, health, and agriculture. In each one, we study the real workflow first, then design systems that fit the institution, the staff, and the local environment.

EdTech
EdTech

Our education work focuses on the systems that keep schools, colleges, and universities functioning day to day. That includes records, admissions, class operations, reporting, attendance visibility, institutional communication, and the administrative workflows that are usually handled with a mix of paper, spreadsheets, and disconnected tools.

In practice, EdTech at ZenLink is not about flashy learning apps. It is about building dependable operational infrastructure for institutions that need clarity, accountability, and better decision-making. We care about whether administrators can trust the data, whether staff can use the system without constant retraining, and whether the platform still works in the realities of Nigerian campuses and schools.

Focus
School and university operations
Problems
Records, reporting, access, coordination
Primary Users
Administrators, staff, institutions
HealthTech
HealthTech

Our HealthTech direction is centered on clinical information flow and service delivery. We are interested in how facilities capture patient information, manage referrals, track treatment history, monitor operational bottlenecks, and maintain continuity of care even when staffing, connectivity, or equipment are inconsistent.

For us, HealthTech means building tools that reduce friction for real healthcare workers, not adding complexity to already strained systems. A useful health platform must help clinics and hospitals document better, respond faster, and generate information leadership can act on. If it does not improve patient care and operational visibility at the same time, it is not solving the right problem.

Focus
Clinical operations and information flow
Problems
Patient records, referrals, reporting, care continuity
Primary Users
Clinics, hospitals, care teams
AgricTech
AgricTech

Our AgricTech work looks at the operational side of agriculture: how produce moves, how farmers and cooperatives track inputs and output, how buyers see reliable supply, and how information gaps create waste, mistrust, or poor pricing. Agriculture is not only a field problem. It is also a coordination and data problem.

That means the systems we design in this area aim to improve traceability, market access, visibility across the chain, and decision-making for both producers and organisers. The goal is not to digitise agriculture for its own sake. The goal is to build practical infrastructure that helps more people plan better, lose less, and transact with more confidence.

Focus
Agricultural operations and supply visibility
Problems
Traceability, market access, cost tracking, losses
Primary Users
Farmers, cooperatives, aggregators

EdTech
Hall Monitor

Hall Monitor is a real-time lecture hall occupancy and visibility system designed for university environments. It helps institutions know what is happening inside teaching spaces as it happens, instead of relying on assumptions, delayed reporting, or fragmented manual checks.

The product is built around operational clarity. Students check in, administrators see live usage patterns, and leadership gets data that supports better planning around space allocation, attendance verification, and classroom management. It is a practical institutional tool, not just a student-facing app.

How We Work

From observation to deployment

01
Identify the friction
We start inside the institution. We observe the actual workflow, document where systems break down, and only then define the right system.
02
Design for the context
Every interface is designed for the real user: an administrator with limited time, unreliable internet, and an older device. Simplicity is a hard requirement.
03
Prove it with a pilot
We deploy to a single institution first, measure everything, fix what breaks. Only then do we scale.
04
Institutionalise it
A good system without institutional buy-in goes nowhere. We work with leadership to integrate our tools into policy, not just workflow.
